By Andrea Perez

The U.S. Latino community is no longer just a demographic trend—it is the engine of the American economy. Now 68 million strong, Latinos represent one-in-five Americans and account for more than half of the nation's total population growth since 2000. The economic impact is staggering: in 2023, the U.S. Latino GDP hit $4 trillion, effectively making it the 5th largest economy in the world if measured as a standalone nation.

While public discourse often narrows its focus to migration, the data reveals a far more significant reality: the Latino community is an indispensable economic titan. By replacing outdated stereotypes with hard data, we see that Latino hard work and entrepreneurship aren’t merely cultural traits—they are the bedrock of modern American prosperity.

This is a story of a community reframed, moving beyond simple demographics to emerge as a primary driver of national wealth. With a workforce of 32 million and a labor participation rate that consistently outpaces the national average, this community has become the nation’s backbone. From 5 million Latino-owned businesses to a record $2.5 trillion in annual spending power, the revelation is clear: the future of the American Dream is being fueled by the ambition and unstoppable spirit of its Latino population.

TOTAL LATINO POPULATION IN THE US

Representing 20% of the total population, the 68 million Americans of Latino descent
are the nation's largest racial or ethnic minority.

Latinos are among the fastest-growing racial or ethnic groups in the United States. Between 2000 and 2024, the Latino population nearly doubled, rising from 35.3 million to 68 million. Latinos accounted for more than half of all U.S. population growth during that period.

Today, Latinos are the country’s second-largest racial or ethnic group, making up one-in-five Americans. They are also strikingly diverse, relatively young, mostly U.S. born and increasingly dispersed across the country.

LATINOS ARE MAJOR DRIVERS OF THE U.S. ECONOMY

The U.S. Latino economy reached $4 trillion in 2023, making it the 5th largest
economy in the world if measured independently.

Latinos are an essential force in America’s economy.
The U.S. Latino economy is expected to continue expanding faster than most first-world economies,
supported by population growth, youth, business formation, improvements in educational attainment, and general upward mobility.

THE IMPACT OF HISPANIC-OWNED BUSINESSES
ON THE U.S. ECONOMY

Data from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics show that there are more than 5 million Latino-owned businesses operating in the country, employing more than 3.5 million workers. These businesses contribute over $800 billion per year to the U.S. economy through sales trade. The number of LOBs grew by close to 1 million new businesses from 2018 to 2024. Latino businesses with employees are concentrated in six important industrial sectors: construction, accommodation & food services, professional, scientific, and technical services,administrative and support services, health care and social assistance, and retail.

THE BACKBONE OF THE U.S. WORKFORCE

The Latino workforce reached an average of 32 million workers in 2025, marking a 5.5% year-over-year increase and a 46.5% rise since 2010. The Latino labor force participation rate reached 69%, well above the national average.

The data highlights a significant shift in the American economic landscape, positioning the Latino community as a primary driver of national labor growth. With a workforce expanding to 32 million in 2025, the 46.5% increase since 2010 underscores a long-term trend of rapid demographic integration and economic contribution. Perhaps most telling is the 67% labor force participation rate, which comfortably outpaces the national average. This statistic reflects not just a growing population, but a highly active and engaged demographic that is effectively serving as the "backbone" of the modern U.S. economy.

INVESTING IN THE AMERICAN PROMISE

Latino households are the defining force of the U.S. economy. By 2023, their gross domestic income (GDI)
reached a record $3.1 trillion, a massive increase from $1.2 trillion in 2019.

2023 Total Domestic Income (GDI): $3.1 trillion

This surge in earnings enabled the community to contribute an estimated $815 billion in total taxes, including approximately $520 billion in federal income taxes and over $295 billion in state and local taxes. Even after meeting these substantial tax obligations, Latino households held over $2.5 trillion in pure spending power, driving growth across every major American industry.

Sources: U.S. Census Bureau, Pew Research Center , 2025 Official LDC U.S. Latino GDP Report™
